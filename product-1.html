The Cornucopia design solves the vertical plane: from above, a stream falls in a parabolic arc governed by gravity and bladder pressure. Its angle of approach relative to the basin wall changes depending on user height and distance. A standard flat wall presents a near-perpendicular surface to almost all trajectories ‚Äî guaranteeing splashback.

The Cornucopia applies the isogonal curve problem to the side-view (r‚Äìz plane) cross-section. This technique defines a surface that intersects an entire family of parabolic curves ‚Äî every possible urine trajectory ‚Äî at a constant, acute angle of 30¬∞ or less.

The resulting profile, revolved around the vertical axis, produces a horn-like shape: deep, curved, with high swept side walls. This does two things: it redirects the primary stream downward rather than reflecting it, and the high walls catch any satellite droplets before they escape.

In controlled laboratory testing using high-speed imaging, the Cornucopia geometry alone reduced measurable splashback to just 1.4% of a standard commercial urinal ‚Äî before any surface coating is applied.

Why 30¬∞ Is The Critical Number

Once geometry has redirected 98%+ of fluid away from the user, the surface coating handles the rest. Standard porcelain is actually hydrophilic ‚Äî it attracts and holds water. Urine spreads, sits, and breaks down into ammonia. That's where the smell comes from.

Standard hydrophobic coatings (inspired by the lotus leaf) create microscopic air cushions that repel water. They partially work ‚Äî but a high-pressure stream can collapse the air layer permanently. Surfactants in urine further degrade the chemistry. One scratch from a cleaning brush destroys it.

Our solution draws from the pitcher plant (Nepenthes). Instead of trapping air, it traps a thin layer of liquid lubricant in its surface pores ‚Äî creating a near-frictionless interface that causes insects to slide helplessly into its digestive chamber. SLIPS (Slippery Liquid-Infused Porous Surfaces) replicates this with a perfluorinated lubricant locked into nano-porous ceramic.
